# My Tech Health Plan

## Current Baseline
- Sleep: ~6 hours per night (want 7–8)
- Exercise: Minimal intentional movement during the week
- Screen breaks: Infrequent; combining a full-time Java developer role with OMSCS prep and local LLM tinkering means prolonged, uninterrupted screen exposure.
- Stress level: 7/10 (juggling the transition to the new role, upcoming coursework, and personal projects)

## My Top Risk Zones
1. Sedentary lifestyle – Commuting in the Camry, sitting at the desk in Woodlawn all day, and coming home to sit for gaming or writing fanfiction leads to 10+ hours of sitting.
2. Eye strain – Staring at screens for work, then immediately transitioning to Minecraft or writing at home without visual breaks.
3. Burnout risk – Trying to balance a career, a part-time master's degree, and intensive technical hobbies without dedicated mental recovery time.

## My Commitments

### Eye Health
- 5-minute break every 30 minutes using the 20-20-20 rule.
- Set a desktop timer to remind me.
- During breaks: look away from the monitor, close my eyes, or stretch my neck.

### Movement
- 20-minute walk around Ellicott City each weekday morning before commuting.
- Stand up and stretch every 60 minutes during work and study sessions.
- Posture check: ensure my workstation is ergonomically set up to prevent back and shoulder pain.

### Sleep
- Hard stop on all screen-based activities (coding, OMSCS prep, LLM training, writing) by 9:30 PM.
- Bed by 10:30 PM.
- Wind down by reading a physical book or relaxing away from devices.
- Disconnect completely one evening per week.

### Nutrition
- Utilize weekend cooking projects (like prepping cheese sauces or experimenting with meat textures) to meal-prep lunches for the workweek so I don't skip meals while focused.
- Keep a water bottle at the desk at all times; aim for 8 glasses a day.
- Refill water every time I get up for a screen break.

### Mental Health
- Spend dedicated, screen-free time talking with my mom or brother at least twice a week.
- Engage in cooking or food science experiments as a hands-on, non-digital creative outlet.
- If feeling overwhelmed by the master's workload or job, step away for a walk instead of forcing it.

### Caffeine
- Limit coffee/caffeine to morning hours.
- No caffeine after 2:00 PM to ensure it doesn't disrupt sleep.

## How I'll Remember
- Phone alarms and desktop reminders for breaks and lunch.
- Weekly check-in every Sunday evening to review this plan.
- Share this plan with my brother so he can ask how I'm doing with it.

- Stopping screen time by 9:30 PM, especially when deep into a coding project or outlining a new crossover chapter. Setting an automatic screen dimmer or physical alarm in another room might make it more doable to break the focus.

Which of your risk zones feels most serious? What could happen if you don't address it over the next 3 months?
- Burnout. If not addressed, the combination of full-time work and Georgia Tech coursework could become unsustainable, leading to exhaustion and a drop in performance across the board.

Who will hold you accountable? 
- My family at home.

- Using my existing interest in cooking to ensure I have properly prepped, nutritious lunches for the week.
